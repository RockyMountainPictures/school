ABOUT ME

Hi,

this is me. This is also the world wide web and I like my privacy.

This was a previous exercise that I had to do in grade 9, so I've tweaked it since and will keep updating it every year.

Hello World.

I've always been interested in programming, but never delved into it much. It seems too complicated from an outsider's point of view. Anyway, you can call me Max. I attend a school in the SD43 cachement. I've always been interested in technology and how it works. I assembled my first computer at age 12, deconstructed a TV for a project in grade 7, and started constructing this website for another project in grade 8.

While I like the intracasies of tech, I also enjoy gaming on said tech. Minecraft is still one of my favourite games to spend my free time. And since it's a sandbox game, there are endless possibilities on how to spend it! Over the 5+ years I've played it, I've spent over 1000 hours destroying, building, killing, and creating.

Sometimes, you just have to relax and spend time away from technology. I always love a nice walk in the forest and enjoying nature. Whenever my family and I go on a big trip, I like bring my camera along and take pictures of the scenery. Photography is a hobby I will always hold onto, and it keeps stories in the photos you take. My dream job would be a nature photographer for the National Geographic or a similar company.

I also do other activities such as mountain biking, skiing, running, badminton and squash.

I'm not a thing for quotes, but this one from Marques Brownlee (MKBHD) is one that has stuck with me. It seems easy to be in control your life and schedule, but it's easier said than done. At least once a day, something that I wanted to do always gets pushed back because of someone else's plans, whether it's personal or school related. But it's okay to have something slip through and mess up your day. It gives life an interesting twist and helps you prepare for other deviations that will occur in the future.

I watch YouTube too much, but there is a lot of content that I love. One of my favourite videos that I've come across is one from a fellow photographer/videographer. Johnny Harris works for Vox, but also has is own Youtube channel where he does some interesting takes on photo/video related topics. One of his best videos is his opinion on famous photos. It's honestly worth a watch if you are someone that wants to take that famous picture themselves.
