My Digital Footprint

An assignment focused on how I use the internet

My digital footprint is pretty minimal. I have a public YouTube account, which says that I like Minecraft and photography (by my channel banner) and my friend’s channels. I also have one news article written with my picture and name within it because we donated our family’s boat to help research the Winnipeg. I also have a Microsoft, Steam, Google+, and Discord accounts, but all of those are private and nobody see’s anything but my avatar. I think people would think I’m a gamer, do photography in my spare time and is very generous, at least from the public data.

I think that my digital footprint would affect my future opportunities in getting a job and hurt my reputation or my personal brand (if I owned a business). It would also affect my post-secondary opportunities. If I was trying to get a job, the company would most likely look into my digital footprint, and according to a study, 63% of employers turn down applicants because of their digital footprint. They do this to not ruin the business’s reputation. My digital footprint would also affect my post-secondary opportunities because they also don’t want to accept any students that could harm the university or college’s reputation.

To limit my digital footprint, if I were to open a social media account is to set everything to private or just to my friends. I would also think about what and why I am posting something, to see if it’s appropriate and acceptable. I would also use a second email to use for my social accounts and lastly, I would Google myself every few months to see if my digital footprint has changed.

To what I would pass on to other students is to keep track and limit social media, because its permanent. I would show them how damaging the consequences are if you abuse the use of it and tell them to use it moderately.
